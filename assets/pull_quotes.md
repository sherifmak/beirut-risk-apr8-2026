# Pull quotes

Short lines to use as Substack pull quotes, callouts, or social-media
shares. **Every headline claim here includes "per capita" in the subject
clause**, because the qualifier is load-bearing and a hostile reader
will strip any phrasing that relies on a trailing modifier.

---

## The headline claim (safe to use as a standalone)

> **On a per-capita basis, Tuesday in Beirut carried a daily risk of death
> roughly comparable to what the average New Yorker faced on September 11,
> 2001.**

> **Per capita, a Greater Beirut resident's daily risk on Tuesday was about
> half of what the average New Yorker faced on 9/11 — and about three
> quarters on a city-proper basis.**

> **The arithmetic is what it is: Beirut Apr 8 2026 and NYC 9/11 sit in the
> same numerical neighborhood on a per-capita daily-risk basis.**

---

## The port explosion anchor (for a Lebanese audience)

> **For the average Greater Beirut resident, Apr 8 2026 was within ~20% of
> August 4, 2020 on the same metro-area basis.** Same city, same
> denominator, same order.

> We all know exactly where we were at 6:07 PM on August 4, 2020. Tuesday
> was a day of that shape.

---

## The "not a moral claim" disarm

> **This is a numerical comparison, not a moral one.** Two events can
> produce similar per-capita daily-risk numbers and still be categorically
> different in their causes, contexts, and ethical status. What follows is
> a statement about arithmetic, not a claim about equivalence.

---

## The methodology-as-emotional-self-defense

> The honest thing to do with a hard feeling is to try to put bounds
> around it — not to make it smaller, but to make it **the right size**.

> I computed it because the alternative was to sit with an
> undifferentiated *bad* I couldn't reason about.

---

## The closing line (safe for the bottom of the post)

> I didn't feel better afterward. But I understood what I was afraid of
> with a little more precision. That was what I came for.

## Long pull quote (sidebar / inset box)

> I computed it because the alternative was to sit with an

## Caveats to quote alongside the headline

> This is a citywide average. It is the wrong number for any individual
> person's safety — including anyone you love.

> Residents of a block that took a direct hit faced risk 100–1,000× higher
> than the citywide mean. Residents of untargeted neighborhoods faced
> close to a normal day. The average belongs to neither.

> The Lebanese Ministry of Public Health says the 300+ figure is
> preliminary and expected to rise. Every number here is a lower bound.

## ❌ Quotes that were killed in a previous draft — DO NOT USE

The following lines from earlier drafts are too scissoring-vulnerable on
social media (they rely on a trailing qualifier that will get stripped in
reshares). They have been removed:

- ~~"Tuesday was a 9/11 for a city the size of Beirut."~~
  Missing qualifier: "for a city the size of" is easily dropped, leaving
  a bare "Tuesday was a 9/11" that reads as a claim about total casualties.

- ~~"302 and 372 are the same number."~~
  They aren't — they differ by 23%. Use precise language instead.

- ~~"Within a rounding error of August 4, 2020."~~
  17% is not a rounding error. Use "within ~20%" or "in the same
  neighborhood."

If you need a one-liner, use one of the per-capita headline claims above.
